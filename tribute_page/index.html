Desmond Thomas Doss

The harmless soldier of war

The life of Desmond Thomas Doss

Service years
  • February 7, 1919 - Desmond Thomas Doss was born in Lynchburg Virginia, U.S.
  • April 1, 1942 - Joined the army and trained with the reactivated 77th infantry division.
  • May 21, 1945 - He was wounded and evacuated aboard the USS mercy.
Post war
  • August 17, 1942 - Married to Dorothy Pauline Schutte.
  • October 12, 1945 - Received the medal of honor.
  • July 1, 1993 - Remarried to Frances May Duman.
  • March 23, 2006 - Died after being hospitalised due to difficulty in beathing.
  • April 3, 2006 - Buried in the Chattanooga national cemetery
Recognitions
  • 1951 - Camp Desmond T. Doss was created in Grand Ledge, Michigan, to help train young Seventh-day Adventist men for service in the military. The camp was active throughout the Korean and Vietnam Wars before the property was sold in 1988.
  • July 10, 1990 - A section of Georgia Highway 2 between US Highway 27 and Georgia Highway 193 in Walker County was named the "Desmond T. Doss Medal of Honor Highway.
  • March 20, 2000 - Desmond Thomas Doss appeared before the Georgia House of Representatives and was presented a special resolution honoring his heroic accomplishments on behalf of the country.
  • July 4, 2004 - A statue of Doss was dedicated at the National Museum of Patriotism in Atlanta, Georgia, which remained until the museum's closure in July 2010.
  • May 2007 - A statue of Doss was dedicated at Veterans Memorial Park in Collegedale, Tennessee.
  • July 2008 - The guest house at Walter Reed Army Medical Center in Washington, D.C., was renamed Doss Memorial Hall.
  • August 30, 2008 - A two-mile stretch of Alabama Highway 9 in Piedmont was named the "Desmond T. Doss Sr. Memorial Highway".
  • October 25, 2016 - The city of Lynchburg, Virginia, awarded a plaque in his honor to Desmond T. Doss Christian Academy.
  • February 7, 2017 - PETA posthumously honored Doss with a Hero to Animals award in recognition of his lifelong commitment to vegetarianism.
  • May 7, 2019 - The U.S. Army Health Clinic-Schofield Barracks in Hawaii was renamed the Desmond T. Doss Health Clinic.
  • October 12, 2020 - The Lynchburg Virginia Area Veterans Council dedicated a plaque at his former childhood home to commemorate the Desmond T. Doss Veterans Home. The home is Doss's birthplace and is now used as a homeless and displaced veterans shelter.
